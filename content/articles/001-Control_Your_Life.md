Today I saw Anthropic's Claude Code released a little demo video about scanning receipts. Have to admit, very impressive. It can basically replace all the receipt scanning apps on the market. At the same time, as long as you have enough patience to carefully modify the prompts, I think it can replace LedgerLens too.

In the past year of 2025, Agent Skills, autonomous driving, and robots have thrown many people who see the future ahead into panic. The skills we once spent days, weeks, and months learning are worthless in front of it — whether it's structure, memory, or speed, we simply can't compete. It's no exaggeration to say that the difference in intelligence between me and a donkey is just noise in front of AI.

Our world is indeed being changed, but on the other hand, we're also unleashing enormous potential. In terms of granularity, I believe AI will bring massive change. In the pre-Windows era, how many people still remember that calculating interest rates required opening a dictionary table to look things up? And in just twenty years, all coarse-grained data and models have become extremely detailed and continuous. If you're not in industry, you might not be familiar with Just-In-Time, but the information revolution of the past two decades has made enterprise production and management very data driven with increasingly fine granularity.

The emergence of AI will take this to the next level. In the past, many things simply couldn't be recorded — not because people didn't want to, but because the cost was too high. Before AI, I'd never seen anyone track expenses down to the item level of each receipt (except my grandma, who would hand-write every single expense from the week after church every Sunday). Think about it:

- Very few people organize their scattered thoughts into structured notes in real-time.
- Very few people categorize photo albums that haven't been touched in over a decade.
- Very few people archive all the files, drafts, and scanned documents that haven't been touched in three years.

These things are small and trivial. While they have potential value, almost no one has the energy to do them consistently.

Now, AI suddenly makes these things feasible.

- A receipt can be automatically broken down to item level.
- A voice thought can instantly become a structured note.
- Thousands of photos can be automatically archived by AI Agent according to time, location, and people.
- Years of unorganized files can be batch scanned, categorized, and indexed.

In other words, AI is powerful enough to let us structure the data of our lives to a new level. In this process, our lives become increasingly organized. When information becomes sortable, searchable, and reviewable, many originally chaotic things are no longer so stressful. We don't need to record everything, organize everything — with AI's assistance, we can stress free retrieve it all. At that point, we can redirect our energy back to life, spend more time with family, have more peaceful time to get through weekends that are no longer anxious.

May you regain a sense of control over your life.
